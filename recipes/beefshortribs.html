Beef Short Ribs

Pressure Cooker Beef Short Ribs with Red Wind and Chile

Description

My favorite pressure cooker beef short ribs recipe. The complex flavors come from the combination of prunes, leeks, fennel and chipotle chile. Quick and convenient.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt, more as needed
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 to 4 pounds bone-in beef short ribs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or other fat (like bacon fat or duck fat), more as needed
  • 3 leeks, whites only, chopped
  • 2 large fennel bulbs, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chipotle chile powder
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 cup dry red wine, such as Merlot, Pinot Noir, Chianti or Cabernet Sauvignon
  • ½ cup pitted prunes, diced
  • Fennel fronds or sliced scallions, or both, for serving

Steps

  1. Rub salt, coriander and pepper all over beef and let marinate in refrigerator for 1 hour, or, ideally, overnight.
  2. Sear beef on cast iron skillet on medium high until evenly browned on all sides, about 2 minutes per side in batches. Transfer them to pressure cooker.
  3. Add leeks, fennel and pinch of salt to hot pan and cook until soft, about 8 minutes, then add garlic, chile powder and tomato paste; cook until fragrant, 1 to 2 minutes. Pour in wine. Add prunes and beef to pressure coooker
  4. Cover, then cook for 50 minutes on high pressure. Manually release pressure. If sauce seems thin, pull out beef pieces and reduce sauce using sauté function.
  5. Refrigerate overnight and skim fat
  6. Garnish with fennel fronds or scallions.
